What Is NoterBox?

NoterBox is a Personal Cloud appliance

  • Capture and save information to NoterBox. Find it later through a powerful search engine.
  • What type of information? Notes, files, web-clips, journal entries, reminders. Whatever feels important.
  • NoterBox becomes your vault. Your life-stream. Tells your story.

Sounds Boring?

We believe that every time you write something down, you are investing in your future.

The Problem:

- People store long-term information in several locations: Email, Dropbox, Google Drive, note-taking apps like SimpleNote or Tomboy, and a thousand other places (like Desktop?).

- Those locations are "specialized" in storing a single type of information (usually text, files or pictures only).

- Information is spread across several services. Example: you can use Google Drive to store documents, and Google Keep to store notes.

- Org mode? Good, but not for everyone. Evernote? Brilliant, but people are still looking for a different "vision".

- In time, data becomes difficult to find & manage. "Moments" are lost.

Meet NoterBox

- NoterBox helps store, document, keep track of data & events

- NoterBox makes it easy to attach any type of files to your notes and manage your documents

- Accept data from several sources: capture via web clipper, drag-and-drop on desktop or email

- Any type of data can become a journal-style entry or a reminder

- Have all your information available when needed - even years later

- NoterBox unifies all your data in one single place where you can easily manage and find it. A single centralized storage location to "rule them all"

What Is The "Secret Sauce"?

With emerging technologies - a few attempts of building similar apps are noticeable. They either offer partial features or are lacking features. Regardless of the stack used, we feel none have the "right balance" as a whole.
What is the "Secret Sauce" in our vision?
  1. Users should have the option to store data on their own server/location, besides NoterBox servers. One of the most requested features.
  2. Easy data capture from multiple sources with emphasize on desktop integration and web clipping functionallity.
  3. Clear separation between entries and attachments. Seems obvious but existing apps do not have this. That includes Evernote as well.
  4. Cross-platform availability: Web, Desktop, Mobile.

Key Differentiators

  • Choice: NoterBox provided storage OR user's own storage
  • Provision: Attach files to your entries. Clear separation between attachments and content
  • Execution: Complex "under the hood" but easy to use. Simple without being simplistic. Clear & clean UI
  • Versatility: It can be a note-taking app, journal or a fully featured document management solution. Or all three
  • Delivered as a personal device/appliance would be the ultimate goal. Information can be stored on premises and accessed from anywhere

Why Now?

It is important to note the lack of exiting solutions.

Companies like Google, Facebook, or Twitter are successful (among other reasons) because they:
  • are helping people search public data;
  • rely on the human factor of sociability (reach out & share news, updates)

NoterBox is doing the opposite: help people organize and access their private data. If done right, this can be a huge market with untapped potential.
